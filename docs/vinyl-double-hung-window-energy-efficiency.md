# Vinyl Double Hung Window Thermal Performance and Air Infiltration Specification

## Overview
This document defines the energy performance parameters and structural characteristics of vinyl double hung windows engineered for residential and commercial applications. Thermal efficiency is achieved through multi‑chambered frame profiles, interlocking weatherstripping, and low‑E coated glazing units.

## Performance Parameters

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| U‑Factor | ≤ 0.30 (achievable 0.27) |
| Air Infiltration – Industry Minimum (AAMA) | ≤ 0.30 cfm/ft² |
| Air Infiltration – Good Performance | ≤ 0.10 cfm/ft² |
| Air Infiltration – Premium (Passive House‑ready) | ≤ 0.06 cfm/ft² |
| Air Infiltration – Ultra‑Premium (Triple‑seal) | ≤ 0.03 cfm/ft² |
| Frame Thickness | 3–4 inches |
| Sash Chamber Count | Minimum 3 |
| Main Frame Chamber Count | 4–5 |

## Material Configuration

| Component | Material |
|-----------|----------|
| Frame & Sash Profile | Extruded PVC (vinyl) with multiple isolated air chambers |
| Corner Joints | Fusion‑welded |
| Reinforcement (optional, for large openings) | Structural aluminum inserts |
| Weatherstripping | Interlocking rigid fin engaging with channel on meeting rail and side jambs |
| Glazing | Dual‑ or triple‑pane with low‑E coating |

## Structural Characteristics

- Frame and sash contain multiple isolated air chambers acting as thermal breaks.
- Fusion‑welded corner joints eliminate air gaps at corners.
- Structural aluminum inserts available for reinforcement in larger openings.
- Interlocking weatherstripping creates a labyrinth seal, reducing air leakage.
- Cam‑action locks pull sashes tighter against jamb seals, maintaining compression over operational cycles.

## Thermal / Energy Performance

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Effective U‑Factor (achievable) | 0.27 |
| Air Leakage (premium interlocking seal) | ≤ 0.06 cfm/ft² |
| HVAC Load Reduction (relative to code‑minimum windows when meeting ENERGY STAR Most Efficient criteria) | 15–25 % |

## Applicable Standards

- NFRC (National Fenestration Rating Council) – independent testing label
- AAMA (American Architectural Manufacturers Association) – air infiltration rating levels
- ENERGY STAR Most Efficient – criteria for maximum energy performance

## Engineering Notes

- For hung windows, air infiltration is the most critical performance metric affecting occupant comfort and HVAC load.
- Interlocking weatherstripping is required to achieve air leakage values ≤ 0.06 cfm/ft²; standard pile or bulb seals typically result in ≥ 1.0 cfm/ft².
- Specifying dual‑ or triple‑pane units must be paired with a tight frame seal; high‑performing glass is ineffective if the frame leaks.
- The NFRC label provides independent verification of U‑factor and air infiltration values; always require it for specification.
