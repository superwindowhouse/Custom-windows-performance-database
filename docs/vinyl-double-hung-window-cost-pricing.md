## Structural Characteristics

- Frame constructed from multi‑chamber extruded vinyl profiles (ASTM D4726)
- Fusion‑welded corner joints
- Integrated balance systems (spiral or block‑and‑tackle) for sash operation
- Tilt‑latch hardware for both sashes (double‑hung configuration)
- Custom sizes require heavier‑gauge steel or aluminum reinforcements to maintain DP50 or higher
- Standard frame width up to 5 ft x 6 ft; reinforcement added for larger spans

## Engineering Notes

- Factory‑direct pricing excludes installation labor, dealer markup, and regional overhead. Request FOB origin (ex‑works) for net material cost.
- Double‑hung windows cost 15–25% more than single‑hung equivalents due to additional balance mechanisms, tilt‑latch hardware, and sash construction.
- Consolidate custom sizes (non‑standard dimensions) into a single production run to minimize tooling changeover costs ($50–$100 per unique size order).
- Bulk order discounts: 50–99 units 12–15%, 100–299 units 15–20%, 300+ units 20–25%. Small batch orders incur 8–12% administrative and shipping overhead.
- Impact‑resistant glass is only cost‑effective for coastal high‑wind zones; inland commercial projects can use standard dual‑pane with tempered inner lite (60% cost saving).
- White vinyl is the most economical frame color; woodgrain laminates or integral colors add $8–$15 per window.
- Negotiate breakage allowance (standard 2–3% overage; can be reduced to 1% with factory QA).
